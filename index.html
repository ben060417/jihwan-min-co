Thesis

Two markets, one corridor.

The most interesting money of the next decade does not sit still. It moves between Seoul and New York, between Korean institutions looking outward and American markets looking for capital.

Most firms working along this corridor are drowning in unstructured data: lease agreements that take days to read, client records scattered across formats, financial documents that resist analysis. Jihwan Min & Co. helps boutique CRE and wealth management firms turn that raw material into clean, usable intelligence.

Alongside that work, this desk publishes independent research on the capital markets that make the corridor move, written in plain language by someone who reads both sides of the Pacific in their own tongue.
